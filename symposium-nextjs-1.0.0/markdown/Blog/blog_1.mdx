A **Personal Loan** is one of the most flexible financial solutions available today. Whether you need funds for medical emergencies, marriage expenses, travel, education, or debt consolidation, a personal loan helps you meet your needs without collateral.

Below is a complete and transparent explanation of how personal loan approval works.

---

## 1. Credit Score (CIBIL Score)

**What it is:**  
A **CIBIL score** is a 3-digit number ranging from **300 to 900** that represents your credit history and repayment behavior.

**Why it matters:**  
This is the **most important factor** in personal loan approval.

**Ideal Score:**  
- **750+** → Excellent (Fast approval & lowest interest rates)  
- **700–749** → Good (Approval likely at standard rates)  
- **600–699** → Average (Approval possible but at higher interest rates)  

**Important Note:**  
Even if your CIBIL score is slightly low, you may still be eligible for a personal loan depending on income, employer, and existing obligations.

---

## 2. Income & Repayment Capacity

Lenders carefully assess your income to ensure you can comfortably repay the EMI.

### For Salaried Individuals:
- Minimum monthly income usually ranges between **₹15,000 – ₹25,000**
- Salary requirement may vary based on city and employer profile

### For Self-Employed Individuals:
- Annual business turnover
- Last **2 years’ Income Tax Returns (ITR)**

**Why this is important:**  
Banks ensure that after all expenses, you still have sufficient income left to repay the loan without financial stress.

---

## 3. Employment Stability

**For Salaried Applicants:**
- Minimum **6 months to 1 year** in the current job is preferred

**For Self-Employed Applicants:**
- Business continuity of **2–3 years** is generally required

**Why lenders care:**  
Frequent job changes or unstable income increase the risk of default, which can lead to rejection or higher interest rates.

---

## 4. Existing Debt (Debt-to-Income Ratio)

**The Golden Rule:**  
Your total EMIs (existing loans + new personal loan EMI) should **not exceed 50%** of your monthly income.

**Example:**  
If your monthly income is ₹40,000, total EMIs should ideally stay below ₹20,000.

**Tip:**  
If you already have multiple running loans, consider:
- Loan consolidation  
- Balance transfer to reduce EMI  

Our experts at Speedy Loan Finance Services can guide you here.

---

## 5. Documentation (KYC & Financial Proofs)

### KYC Documents (Mandatory):
- **Aadhaar Card**
- **PAN Card**

### Income Proof – Salaried:
- Last **3 months’ salary slips**
- Last **3–6 months’ bank statements**

### Income Proof – Self-Employed:
- Last **2 years’ ITR**
- Balance Sheet / Profit & Loss statement

Having proper documents ensures **faster processing and quicker disbursal**.

---

If you already maintain a **Savings Account or Salary Account** with a bank:

- You may receive **Pre-Approved Personal Loan Offers**
- Benefits include:
  - Zero documentation

  - Same-day disbursal

This is one of the easiest ways to get a personal loan.

## Summary Checklist for Customers

Before applying for a personal loan, ask yourself these **3 important questions**:

- **What is my current CIBIL score?**
- **Do I have valid income proof (Salary slip or ITR)?**
- **Do I already have too many loans running?**

If the answer looks positive, your chances of approval are high.
